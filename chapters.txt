Chapter 1 – Introduction
---
Takeda wears a dark-colored T-shirt layered under a jacket and a hat, while Arita wears a white T-shirt with a jacket featuring pockets on both sides and striped details around the collar and cuffs.
---
Takeda is packing up his belongings to move into the apartment where he and Arita will be living together. His current room number is 406.

Arita borrows a car from the flower shop where he works and jokes that Takeda should pay him 20,000 yen for helping with the move.

Since Takeda’s apartment is on the fourth floor with no elevator, it takes at least three trips to carry everything down. Arita jokes again that it would be easier to just throw the boxes downstairs instead. In total, they move five boxes.

While bringing the boxes to the car, Takeda notices what seems to be a white cat hiding underneath something nearby.

Inside the car, Takeda sits on the left side while Arita drives on the right. Arita teases him again, saying, “You really look at home in that driver’s seat,” before jokingly deducting another 10,000 yen.

Their new apartment is located on the highest floor, near the far right end of the hallway.

While unpacking, Arita asks about the gas stove. Takeda thought Arita was bringing it, while Arita assumed Takeda would. Takeda was supposed to bring a toaster instead, but due to the misunderstanding, they somehow end up with two toasters.

Later, Takeda takes the cardboard boxes away and notices a child running toward their mother in the hallway. Watching the scene makes him smile.

Takeda works as a Japanese teacher in the humanities field.

The two discuss what they should eat after moving in, bringing up hikkoshi soba, the traditional “moving-in soba” from the Edo period. They also mention toshikoshi soba, the noodles eaten on New Year’s Eve. Takeda shares his own interpretation of the tradition: that a new beginning should be smooth and long-lasting, just like a long soba noodle.

Arita then talks about uchiiri soba, or “raid soba,” associated with the 47 Ronin and the Ako Incident, traditionally eaten on December 14th. He also mentions teuchi soba, handmade soba prepared using traditional methods.

On the balcony, there is an outdoor unit with two small plants placed to the left side.

Chapter 2
---
Takeda wears a loose white long-sleeved T-shirt, later adding a backpack when they go out shopping. Arita wears a dark T-shirt under a plain jacket without pockets or striped details.
----
It is the weekend.

Takeda tells Arita that his room has no curtains. Arita admits he did not notice either, and Takeda says that if he had known beforehand, he would have brought some himself. Curious about how Takeda managed to sleep the previous night, Arita asks him about it. Takeda explains that he hung a towel over the window, and surprisingly, it worked.

The two decide to go shopping, and Takeda wonders where they can buy curtains.

Before leaving, Takeda also comments that there are barely any dishes in the apartment—or perhaps Arita simply does not own many. Arita casually explains that he usually eats straight from the pot, then stores leftovers in containers afterward since it means less cleaning and keeps the food warm longer. Takeda is visibly shocked and insists that will not do.

They split up their shopping goals: Takeda will look for curtains, while Arita searches for plates and a microwave.

They head to a store called Nason.

At the supermarket, Takeda becomes nervous that one of his students might spot him there, since the school where he teaches is located nearby.

As they search for household items, Takeda keeps asking Arita to buy additional things as well. Arita looks exhausted and speechless, though he still goes to look for them anyway. Eventually, they split directions: Takeda heads straight while Arita turns left.

Takeda’s first stop is supposed to be the curtain section, but he accidentally ends up among the pillows instead.

While looking at pillows, he is suddenly spotted by two of his students: Tsukiyama, with dark hair, and Tachibana, who is blonde. They are shopping for stationery and other supplies and are surprised to see him there.

Tachibana asks whether Takeda came alone or with someone else, the two seemingly assuming he might be shopping with a girlfriend. Takeda wants to say he came with Arita, yet hesitates because he does not know how to properly introduce him to other people.

Meanwhile, Arita arrives at the curtain section but cannot find Takeda. Wondering if he wandered into the neighboring aisle, he overhears the conversation just as Takeda says, “Like them, I’m here with a friend.”

Tsukiyama jokingly wonders whether Takeda even has friends at all, prompting Takeda to reply that teachers are human too. After that, the students say goodbye and leave.

Afterward, Takeda quietly wonders whether calling Arita a “friend” was really the right choice, even though it was the first word that came to mind. He thinks that if he had mentioned they were living together instead, his students would have kept asking questions.

To Takeda, Arita is simply Arita, there has never been a need for a specific word to define their relationship. Yet somehow, for other people, having a label seems deeply important.

While he is lost in thought, Arita suddenly pokes his cheek. The attempt startles Arita less than Takeda himself.

Arita admits he thought the surprise would work better, and Takeda tells him he should think twice before doing things like that. Takeda then notices that Arita actually managed to buy the dishware.

Arita asks why Takeda was wandering around the pillow aisle in the first place, and Takeda sheepishly admits that he got lost and accidentally ran into his students.

Takeda is horrified that the exact situation he feared actually happened and accuses Arita of lying when he said there was nothing to worry about. Arita apologizes before pointing out that the curtain section is over there.

Takeda pauses for a moment, realizing that in the end, the exact label for the relationship between him and Arita may not matter after all.

After finishing their shopping, they go to look at microwaves. Passing by the frozen food section, they decide to buy pizza so Takeda can try it out. In the end, they eat two kinds together: one basic flavor and one topped with mushrooms.

Chapter 3

---
At home, Arita is dressed casually in a short-sleeved white T-shirt while relaxing in bed. Then a dark shirt while working. Takeda in a shirt for working
---

Arita works at Yamaguchi Flower Shop.

Early in the morning, his alarm rings at 5:30 while his phone is still charging nearby.

Half-awake, Arita wonders what the noise is before Takeda apologizes for accidentally waking him up. Arita says it is fine since he needed to get up around this time anyway. Takeda mentions that he has been craving miso soup, though it seems they already ran out of instant miso mix, so he decides he can go without it for now.

Instead, Arita opens the refrigerator and pulls out mixed miso paste, a packet of bonito flakes, and chopped onions, encouraging Takeda to make proper miso soup instead. He casually asks Takeda to make toast for him as well.

As Takeda prepares the soup, he quietly takes in the smell of real miso.

The morning weather forecast says the first half of the day will be clear, though clouds and rain may arrive later in the afternoon. Arita tells Takeda to bring an umbrella just in case.

Takeda thinks he will probably finish work at school early that day and asks whether Arita needs anything from the store. Arita replies that it is nothing urgent, but they only have one egg left, and they also need more instant miso mix.

The two eat breakfast together: Arita has toast topped with a sunny-side-up egg, while Takeda enjoys the freshly made miso soup.

After hearing Arita mention instant miso again, Takeda reacts with a small “huh?” Arita reminds him that they already ran out earlier. Takeda replies that he does not really need instant miso for now when he has Arita around to make the real thing instead, leaving Arita momentarily speechless.

Takeda quickly adds that he does not expect Arita to make it for him every morning.

Since he is running late, Takeda hurriedly heads out for work.

Later, at Yamaguchi Flower Shop, Arita spends the day arranging bouquets and receives a compliment from a customer for his work.

Soon afterward, an older man named Ootani enters the shop. Arita greets him and mentions that hydrangeas are selling especially well because it is their season, then asks whether Ootani would like a bouquet similar to the displayed arrangement.

While waiting, Ootani asks where Ryuu is. Arita explains that Ryuu stepped out to make a call and will probably return soon. Ootani jokes that Ryuu is a terrible business owner for leaving the shop unattended.

Not long after, Ryuu returns and greets both of them.

Ryuu jokingly asks Ootani whether he is buying flowers for his wife again, and Ootani casually answers that he still buys bouquets for her regularly. He then turns the conversation toward Ryuu’s own love life, asking about things with Hinako.

Once Arita finishes the bouquet, Ootani tells him that if he ever gets a girlfriend, he should let them know so they can celebrate it together. Afterward, he says goodbye and leaves the shop.

Once they are alone, Ryuu brings up how difficult it might be to explain to other people that Arita and Takeda are living together.

Arita says he is not really hiding it at all since he already told Ryuu openly about the move. However, he wonders why Ryuu suddenly brought it up.

Ryuu explains that many people would probably find it unusual for two men to live together, though he also believes it is something worth celebrating.

Arita suddenly realizes Ryuu might have assumed that he and Takeda are dating. Shocked, Ryuu hurriedly insists he never directly thought that.

Embarrassed, Ryuu explains that Arita announced the move so suddenly that he started imagining things on his own. He says it would feel perfectly normal if they were students sharing an apartment, but in this case they are two grown men with completely different jobs and seemingly nothing in common, so his thoughts naturally drifted in that direction. He apologizes and clarifies that he understands now that it is not like that.

Arita apologizes as well for never properly explaining the situation.

Ryuu confirms, “So you’re just roommates, right?”

Arita replies, “Yeah.”

Ryuu comments that they are still young enough to share an apartment together, admitting that it makes him a little jealous. However, he soon remembers he has to head to the kindergarten and leaves the shop.

By then, the weather has turned terrible, with heavy rain pouring outside.

Left alone in the flower shop, Arita quietly reflects on Ryuu’s words—that he and Takeda supposedly have nothing in common.

He thinks back to a few days earlier, remembering Takeda introducing him simply as “a friend.”

To Arita, though, their relationship feels different from the way people view it from the outside. It is not something shameful, nor something he wants to hide. The problem is simply that there is no clear word that fully describes what they are to each other.

Neither he nor Takeda has found the right term for it yet.
